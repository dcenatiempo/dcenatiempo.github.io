Hermes by dcenatiempo

Connect the Hermes AI agent to your Google account

Hermes by dcenatiempo is a personal desktop application that lets the Hermes AI agent securely authenticate with your Google account and act on your behalf through the Google APIs. Once you authorize it, you can ask Hermes in plain language to work with your Gmail, Google Calendar, and Google Drive — and it carries out the request using your own account.

What it does

Hermes is an AI assistant that runs on your own computer. On its own it has no way to reach your Google data. Hermes by dcenatiempo is the component that handles the Google OAuth sign-in and API calls, so that when you ask Hermes to do something involving Google, it has the access it needs to do it. Typical requests include:

  • Search, summarize, draft, and send Gmail messages.
  • Read, create, and update events on your Google Calendar.
  • Find, read, and edit files in Google Drive, Docs, and Sheets.

The application is run by an individual on their own machine and operates only on the single Google account that the same individual personally authorizes. It is not a multi-user service.

How it works

  • You install the application on your own computer.
  • You sign in with Google and grant the scopes you want Hermes to use (Gmail, Calendar, and/or Drive).
  • Authorization tokens are stored locally on your device so you don't have to sign in repeatedly.
  • When you make a request, the agent uses those credentials to read or update your Google data. To fulfill a request, relevant content may be sent to a large language model provider that you choose and configure — or kept entirely local if you run a local model.
  • You can revoke access at any time from your Google Account permissions page.

Privacy & data use

Hermes by dcenatiempo's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Google data is used only to fulfill your own requests, is never sold, and is never used for advertising. For full details, see the documents below.
